Presentation of the final project

  • Each group will make a presentation. Ideally, the presentation would be divided among all members of the group.
  • Each presentation should take around 15 minutes. Ideally, 10 minutes are scheduled for the group to show what they have done, and 5 minutes are scheduled for questions and answers. Questions are welcome from everyone.
  • The structure of the presentation is not strictly fixed, nor it is its content. However, there are some suggestions to have similar structures.

Guidelines for the presentation
(suggestions, not mandatory)

  • (*Optional) Group: who are the people in the group.
  • (*Optional) Tools: which tools you used and how you managed to work in a group.
  • Project: short description of the project and the motivation behind.
  • Story: attempts, failures, successes, material, resources, whatever happened in the process. Sometimes what did not work can be as important as what worked!
  • Results: how it works? can you quantify results or show some visualizations?
  • Baselines:
    • is there a simple baseline with which you can compare? It can be as simple as the mean of the data for time series prediction, or a fixed value of 0.5 for the accuracy in a binary classification problem.
    • can you measure human performances on the task? can you personally take some samples from the test set and solve the problem yourself, and quantify the results?
  • Missing: is there something you missed to improve the project? Time, material, knowledge, data, computational power?
  • Future works: do you have an idea how this could be improved or extended?

Sharing is caring

Would you like to share and make the project available for everyone?
  • Code: is the code online, are you willing to share, can you provide a link to a repository that ideally contains some short instructions to reproduce it?
  • Data: is the data public, or it is not possible to share?
  • Presentation: Do you allow me to upload/link the presentation (here I mean the slides) to the website for the next semesters?

QUIZ (15 mins)


1. Can you use transfer learning on your project? To have better results, what would you like the network to have learned before you fine-tune it?
2. Can you think of a way to use end-to-end deep learning on your project? Or rather divide it into smaller steps? Or none of them fits?
3. If you had 6 more months on your projects, what would be your next step? Assuming no money/time limitation, would you like to have more/better data, an expert validating your data, a more complex model, a more powerful machine to iterate the hyperparameter search, some time to test and apply different regularization techniques or something else?
